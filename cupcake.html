Injection Molded Cupcakes

Design a plastic toy from a children’s drawing

skills

mechanical engineering, cad, manufacturing

role

CAD designer

client

Computer Integrated Manufacturing Class


My team of four took a children’s drawing of a smiling cupcake and turned it into a reality. My role was designing and modeling the part and molds. After going through multiple iterations, we reached a simple and elegant design for our toy. We then made the CAM program, manufactured the molds and injection molded 100 of them of them to check for consistency.


Key Learnings

In this project, I gained experience designing for injection molded parts. Seeing the process from ideation through molding, I could apply my knowledge of the process and learn why each rule exists. For example, I accidentally modeled one wall on the cupcake to be thicker than the rest, which caused severe pitting and burning, forcing us to remake one mold. Going through the act of machining the molds and injection molding the parts made me appreciate Designing for Manufacturing and the effect of good or bad design on every person who interacts with the product during manufacturing.


My Work

Modeling

The frosting was a challenge because the shape we wanted could not be injection moldable in a simple two part mold due to the steep angles required. We decided to simplify the top to allow for easier manufacturing and moldability.

Mold Making

I created a two part mold to hold both frosting and cup components. This saved machining time, material cost, and molding steps. I modeled the part and molds using Siemens NX.

Computer Aided Manufacturing

Once the molds were completed, I assisted in the CAM programming. We intentionally added scallops on the outside frosting face to give it texture. We machined the molds on a CNC mill.

Results

The pins and holes of the two parts fit together snugly, which allowed for the cupcake to stay whole. The toys turned out as planned which made our group and the children who drew it up very happy.

Molding Analysis

We molded 100 cupcakes, comparing the thickness of the frosting when cooled for 12 seconds in the machine versus 6 seconds. After doing a regression analysis, we found no statistical difference between the two times. This means the part should be cooled for only 6 seconds, which would save 10 minutes for every 100 cupcakes.
