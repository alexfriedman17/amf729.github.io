Siemens Healthcare

Mechanical Engineering Intern

skills

mechanical engineering, cad, prototyping

role

mechanical engineering intern

client

siemens healthcare


At Siemens, my main responsability was to design and model injection molded covers for a next generation diagnostic imaging system. I worked with industrial designers, mechanical engineers and service technicians to create covers that looked elegant, could be manufactured reliably and would be easy to assemble/dismantle for machine maintenance. After working with complex surfacing in NX to create the exact look the industrial designer wanted, I then worked on splitting and shelling the body into moldable covers. I then designed the mounting hardware, keeping Design for Assembly principles like fastener optimization in mind.

I also 3D printed models of future and past imaging systems used by senior management to determine direction of the project. These models could be adjusted and moved around to simulate the movements of the machine. Finally, I investigated user complaints, conducted testing, analyzed data and wrote an engineering change order.

Due to my non disclosure agreement, I cannot show any images of my work or go into more detail about the projects I worked on.


Key Learnings

The biggest skill I worked on was complex CAD modeling. I needed to make a very robust model as the industrial designer was constantly making edits to the appearance. I worked with surface modeling to get the look needed. I also learned about injection molding best practices and sheet metal bracket design.
