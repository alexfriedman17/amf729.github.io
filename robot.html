Autonomous Robot Design Competition

Autonomous hide and seek robot

skills

robotics, iterative design, prototyping

role

designer

client

northwestern university autonomous robot design competition 2017


The challenge was to design a robot to autonomously avoid a human driven hunter robot. The robot had to balance a cup on its head and navigate through a grid of buckets away from the hunter. My robot won third place using position tracking, phototransistors and bump sensors to navigate the terrain and strategically avoid the hunter.


Key Learnings

Through this competition, I learned advanced arduino control with multiple sensors and motors to manage. As this was my first unguided robotics project, I also learned troubleshooting techniques, designing for future development, and autonomous coding. I was happy with how my robot turned out because although it wasnâ€™t the most complex, it was one of the most reliable and consistent robots.


My Work

Start Simple

I built up my robot, one feature at a time, ensuring that at every stage I had a working robot that behaved predictably. My first version only had the ability to drive randomly, then I added photosensors, then bump sensors, then location tracking and finally hunter robot tracking.

Itterative Design

I made five major versions of the robot, each time adjusting the physical design to better fit the needs of the problem. Using a combination of 3D printing and laser cutting, I could quickly and effortlessly refine my designs.

Technology Used

I built this project with arduino coding on a teensy, using photosensors, push buttons, motor control, HTC Vive lighthouse location sensors and Xbee wireless receivers.

Photosensors

The photosensors detected the hunter by comparing the light level before and after flashing lasers around the robot. The hunter was coated with retroreflective material allowing the lasers to trigger a high on the photosensors.

Bump Sensors

Using push switches with a large shield on top allows for the robot to sense when it hits an obstacle and from what side. This allowed the robot to roam freely and get itself unstuck.

Position Sensors

Using an HTC vive, the robot collected its position and compared it to the location being broadcast and collected by an Xbee receiver. My robot would only move if the hunter robot was within 6 feet.

Next Steps

The feature I planned on implementing next was using the location to strategically navigate the arena. From there the robot would be able to actively avoid the hunter instead of running in a random direction.
