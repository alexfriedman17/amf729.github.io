Smart Soccer Training Device

Autonomous soccer passing and awareness training

skills

mechanical engineering, iterative design, prototyping, human factors, robotics

role

lead mechanical engineer and prototyper

client

northwestern varsity soccer teams


All soccer skills can be practiced alone except for passing. Our challenge was to design a system to train awareness, touch time and pass accuracy with only one or two players. Our system is an arrangement of “smart” targets which alert the user where and when to pass. The system then tracks data on the user and sends the information to their phone for analysis.


Key Learnings

As the lead mechanical engineer and prototyper, I gained experience in balancing cost, speed and effectiveness by deciding when to spend more to buy existing products and when to manufacture parts ourselves. Due to the short design cycle, we manufactured and tested a new prototype most weeks. I also wrote and was awarded a grant from the Norman Design Fund for another round of prototyping materials.


The Design Process

Problem Identification

Our client asked for a product which “gave players more measureable training data.” We discovered what data they need and can’t already get by observing practices, researching current technologies, and interviewing coaches and players. Our group of five determined that the team needs to train not only passing, but awareness and touch time as well, to better simulate a game.

Brainstorm

We brainstormed on the topics of data collection, target design, ball feeding and drill types. We pursued a couple combinations of sensors, target styles, and game layouts.

Initial Mockups

We first made a rough mockup with vibration sensors on a folding goal. I lead the manufacturing of a PVC goal with infrared sensors.

User Testing

We tested the experience with three users and found that custom goals would be required to achieve the structural stability and portability needed for our design. Available systems were either too heavy or flimsy.

Iterative Design

As I lead the prototyping and modifications of our designs, other teammates conducted more user testing and patent research. We explored different goal designs from sheet metal to spring loaded frames to trashcans. The iterative design process lead us to metal and PVC frame with large police style LED light boards and plastic cleats.

Final Prototype

Our final deliverable was four goals with IR sensors, two colors of LED lights and no knockback at speeds up to double the expected passing speed. The goals connected to an app which controlled the drill and recorded the data. I also constructed a custom carrying cart to help transport the product.
